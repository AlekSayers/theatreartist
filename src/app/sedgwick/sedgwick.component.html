LOGLINE

A malevalent ghoul walks the streets of Chicago, embedding itself in the city inhabitants' lives. For some, it inspires fear; for others, its predictable and violent nature presents opportunity.

STORY

THEMES

Sedgwick is about what it's like to live in a city. Suffering is everywhere: violence, drug overdoses, homelessness. But despite high-minded rhetoric, the reality is that most aren't interested or capable of solving these hardships.

  • To most, homelessness is an annoying blight
  • To a few, the proliferation of drugs present opportunity
  • To cable news networks, city violence is a cudgel to wave at liberal policies

If this is how an American city handles societal ills...how would they handle a monster?

CHARACTERS

Lester

Sedgwick is loudly about Lester. While he looks human, he is a ghoul — a haunting physical entity. Lester ambles aimlessly around the streets of Chicago, completely out in the open. Bold Chicagoans take photos of him or follow him until they get bored...or he claims someone.

Q. Why don't police simply kill him?
A. Oh, they tried, with disastrous results. Now he's officially classified a "low-priority national security risk."
Q. Why doesn't the city evacuate?
A. Because Lester only feeds every 4-6 weeks. Ask yourself: would you move from a city of 2 million just because ~10 random people/year were murdered by a supernatural entity?

Chicago

Sedgwick is quietly about the inhabitants of Chicago, and how we react to society's ills. In the pilot episode these perspectives are on full display:

  • Abby, a recent Chicago transplant, finds Lester grimly fascinating — a topic inappropriate for polite conversation.
  • Devon, a Chicago native, resents the way Lester's reputation has become synonymous with the city's.
  • Raj and Vic consider themselves fans of the ghoul. His victims are entertainment fodder, and his violence a source of sardonic pride — the way some people brag about being "from Chi-raq."

EPISODES

Sedgwick was written as an episodic short series. Each episode pivots to show the perspective of a Chicago inhabitant, and how Lester weaves himself into their lives.

Episode 1: Pilot

A recent Chicago transplant finds herself entranced by the city's most macabre attraction: a murderous ghoul.

Episode 2: The Pit

Facing deportation, an Indian graduate school student explores the sites of Chicago during his last days in America.

Episode 3: Rabbit

A group of like-minded youth kick off a plan to draw an enemy into Lester's path.
